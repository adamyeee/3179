Global Population Overview

As of recent estimates, the global population exceeds 8 billion. In terms of population distribution across continents, Asia holds the largest share of the worldâ€™s population by far, followed by Africa and Europe. Other continents, like North America and Oceania, have comparatively smaller populations.

Not considering mortality rate, China and India have the highest amount of births, due to their large population. United States and Germany are also growing rapidly, due to large amount of migrants entering.

Population density varies widely by country, with some nations like Monaco and Singapore exhibiting exceptionally high densities due to their limited land area and large populations. In contrast, countries such as Canada and Australia have low population densities, largely attributable to their vast land areas and relatively smaller populations.

The correlation between median age and fertility rate is significant, as countries with higher median ages typically experience lower fertility rates, which can lead to slower population growth. This relationship is crucial, as an aging population combined with declining birth rates may result in a shrinking workforce and increased pressure on social services.
